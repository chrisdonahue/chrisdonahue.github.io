Chris Donahue

Postdoc, Stanford CS

I am currently on the academic and industry job markets—see my research statement. Please if I might be a good fit for your department or company!

I am a postdoc in the Stanford Computer Science Department working with Percy Liang. Previously, I obtained my PhD from UCSD where I was jointly advised by Julian McAuley (CS) and Miller Puckette (music).

My research goal is to build music technology that enables a broader set of users to engage with music on a deeper level. To this end, I improve machine learning methods for generative modeling of music, audio, and other sequential data, and build real-world interactive music systems which allow a broader audience to harness the power of resultant models.

Concretely, my work has been used to blur the lines between audience and performers in a professional rock concert. to play with Piano Genie, the model behind this experience, right in your browser. I also built a live service called Beat Sage which allows thousands of users per day to transform music into rich interactive content.
